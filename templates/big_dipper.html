The Big Dipper

Asterism

The Big Dipper is one of the most recognizable asterisms in the night sky, known for its distinctive shape that resembles a large ladle or dipper. It is part of the larger constellation of Ursa Major, the Great Bear, and comprises seven bright stars: Alkaid, Mizar, Alioth, Megrez, Phecda, Merak, and Dubhe.
